#myyearup

Year up motivates me every single day. I cannot wait to see my babies face when they see me walk across stage. Even though they are too young to understand , I will feel a sense of accomplishment after completing the program. There are days when i don't want to get up, but i think about how i need to set an example for my daughters and how i can't let them down. I want them to be proud of their mother. Every single day i am reaching my goals of becoming a college graduate. I expect for my kids to go to college and me being the most important person in their life have to set the example. I want to obtain a job to make a livable wage because i not only have to support myself, there are two little girls that depend on me and i'll do anything it takes to better myself for my daughters. They motivate me to want to succeed, to become succesful, to not settle for less. They flipped my world a whole 360 degrees. It is because of them that i went back to school a couple of months ago and worked hard to get my GED because I know that in order to become some one in life i need to go to college and get a degree. This is only the begging. Once i graduate from YearUp, i will continue my college education and receive my Associates degree. I will then complete two more years of college and become the first one in my family to be a college graduate. I let everyone in my community know about this program and together we will close the opportuniy dive one at time......

Nino&Nina.

These two wonderful people pictured on the left is my grandmother and my grandfather. His name is Fortunato and her name is Apolonia. They just recently celebrated their 70 year wedding anniversary, which is rare now-a-days and i'm lucky to have witnessed. They have been married since she was 15 and he was 18. I look up to them so much. They immigrated to this country from Mexico. It amazes me to see how hard they worked to have what they have. My grandpa's first job in the U.S was a dishwasher. He then moved on to look for more work while my grandma stayed in Mexico. She was later able to come to the U.S with her husband. He worked many jobs and was able to save up money to buy a house on Western Ave. They took in my mother because unfortunatley weren't able to have kids of their own. They are more than my grand-parents they are a second mother and father to me. MY grandmother shows me an example of a hardworking women each day. She is always finding things to keep busy even at her 87 years of age. She is the opposite of lazy. She still manages to cook dinner for the family. She is the foundation of my household. They motivate me to take advantage of the opportunites America has to offer. They've come too far in life and i refuse to let them down. I respect these two and they are a good example of a power couple.
